# Why ConferenceAgent Matters

## The Problem
Major scientific conferences — ASCO, AACR, ESMO, ASH, AHA, AASLD — release thousands of abstracts in a short window. R&D, translational, BD, and leadership teams scramble to answer the same questions every cycle: which assets matter, which companies are gaining momentum, which trials could change standard of care, which signals are overhyped, and which are quietly underappreciated. Today this is a manual, last-minute scramble that varies wildly in quality and consumes senior bandwidth for a week or more.

## What ConferenceAgent Does
Automates the first-pass scientific intelligence step. The agent ingests abstracts, ClinicalTrials.gov metadata, PubMed references, press releases, and social-media signals; extracts structured entities (drug, target, company, modality, endpoint); generates quantitative summary tables; and then writes a polished, source-grounded report. Every number in the final report traces back to a specific source row — a QC module enforces this, eliminating LLM-invented statistics.

## Who Benefits
- R&D and translational scientists planning their conference focus
- Business development and competitive-intelligence teams tracking competitor visibility
- Commercial strategy and brand teams reading the field for launch implications
- Leadership preparing pre-conference briefings and post-conference debriefs
- Medical affairs teams identifying KOLs and emerging narratives

## Why Now
Three trends converge: (1) conference abstract data is increasingly structured and downloadable; (2) biomedical NER and modality classification are now mature enough to extract entities at scale; (3) LLMs can synthesize source-grounded narratives reliably **when paired with strict QC** that bars invented numbers. The remaining bottleneck is the integration glue — exactly what ConferenceAgent provides.

## The Bigger Picture
Pre-conference intelligence currently absorbs hundreds of senior-scientist hours per major meeting across the industry, and the resulting reports are inconsistent and often arrive late. A reproducible, source-traceable agent compresses preparation time by 70%+ and frees scientists to focus on judgment — what the data *means* — rather than gathering it. Done well, the same architecture extends from oncology meetings to cardiovascular, hepatology, metabolic, and BD/investor conferences, becoming the organization's standard pre-conference workflow.
